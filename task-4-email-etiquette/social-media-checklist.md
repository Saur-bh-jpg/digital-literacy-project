# Social Media Do's # Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

### ✅ The 5 Do's
1. **Do audit your privacy settings regularly:** Ensure your personal accounts are set to private and you know exactly who can see your posts.
2. **Do keep your professional and personal lives separate:** Use LinkedIn strictly for networking and academic/career updates, while keeping casual updates for private accounts.
3. **Do assume everything is permanent:** Even on disappearing platforms like Snapchat, someone can take a screenshot. Post as if a future employer might see it.
4. **Do showcase your projects:** Share your successful coding projects, hackathon participation, or academic achievements to build a positive digital footprint.
5. **Do verify information before sharing:** Help stop the spread of misinformation by quickly fact-checking articles or news before retweeting or sharing them.

### ❌ The 5 Don'ts
1. **Don't post sensitive personal information:** Never share photos showing your university ID, home address, phone number, or travel plans while you are away.
2. **Don't complain about professors or the university publicly:** Venting online leaves a permanent, searchable record that reflects poorly on your professionalism.
3. **Don't engage in online flame wars:** Avoid getting dragged into aggressive comment sections; it rarely changes minds and often damages your reputation.
4. **Don't use unprofessional handles:** Your email and primary social usernames should be variations of your real name, not nicknames or jokes.
5. **Don't rely entirely on the platform's security:** Don't send highly sensitive data (like passwords or financial info) over social media direct messages, as accounts can be compromised.
