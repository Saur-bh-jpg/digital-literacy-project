# Stay Safe Online: A Guide for College Students

1. **Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money:** A UPI PIN is only required to send money or check your balance. If someone asks you to enter it to receive a payment, it is a scam.
2. **Verify QR codes before scanning:** When paying at local campus vendors or online, check the name displayed on the UPI app before authorizing the payment.
3. **Use strong, unique passwords:** Avoid using your birthdate or phone number. Use a password manager and enable 2FA on your university email and platforms like GitHub.
4. **Beware of urgent, emotional requests:** Phishing messages often claim an account will be suspended or a friend is in urgent need of money. Always pause and verify through a secondary channel.
5. **Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks:** Do not log into your bank account or pay semester fees while connected to an unsecured cafe or hostel Wi-Fi network.
6. **Regularly update your devices:** Keep your laptop's OS and smartphone apps updated to patch known security vulnerabilities.
7. **Limit personal sharing on social media:** Avoid posting real-time location updates or photos of your student ID, which can be easily used for identity theft.
8. **Report incidents immediately:** If you fall victim to cybercrime, do not panic or feel ashamed. Act quickly to freeze accounts and report the fraud.

### **Official Reporting Channels in India:**
* **National Cyber Crime Portal:** cybercrime.gov.in
* **National Cyber Crime Helpline:** Dial 1930 (Available 24x7)
