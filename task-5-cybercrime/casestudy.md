# Case Study: The "QR Code" UPI Scam Targeting Students

**What it is:** UPI fraud occurs when scammers trick victims into authorizing financial transfers from their bank accounts using the Unified Payments Interface. A common variation targeting college students is the "receive money via QR code" scam.

**The Target:** College students buying or selling second-hand items (like engineering textbooks, laptops, or hostel furniture) on online marketplaces or campus WhatsApp groups.

**How it happens (Step-by-Step):**
1. A student posts an ad online to sell a used textbook.
2. The scammer poses as an eager buyer, agrees to the price immediately without negotiating, and insists on paying via UPI right away to "reserve" the item.
3. The scammer sends a QR code or a payment request link to the student on WhatsApp, claiming the student needs to "scan to receive the payment."
4. The student, unaware that scanning a QR code or entering a UPI PIN is *only* required for sending money, scans the code and enters their PIN.
5. Instead of receiving funds, the agreed amount (or more) is instantly deducted from the student's bank account.

**Consequences:** The student loses crucial financial resources meant for semester expenses or daily needs. Because UPI transactions are instant and irreversible, recovering the funds is extremely difficult. This leads to severe financial stress and a lasting loss of trust in digital payment platforms.
